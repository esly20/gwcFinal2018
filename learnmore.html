Homelessness in America

Homelessness is a major issues in America. In NYC, since the 1900's the number of people who are homeless has skyrocketed. In June 2018, there were approximately 61,421 people occupying shelters each night. Of those 61,421 people, 22,266 were children in families, 22,256 were adults that have families, and 16,899 were single adults. The number of homeless people sleeping in shelters has increased by 85% over the past ten years. The main cause of homelessness in NYC is unaffordable housing. Other major reasons include - eviction, doubled-up or severely overcrowded housing, domestic violence, job loss, and hazardous housing conditions.
