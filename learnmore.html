FACTS

Homelessness is a big issues in America. We always see them but we never really know their story. Here in NYC, the number of homeless people has skyrocketed, reaching one of it highest levels since the 1930s Great Depression ; studies show that in June 2018, there were approximately 61,421 people in Shelters every night. Of those 61,421 people, 22,266 were children in families, 22,256 were adults that have families, and 16,899 were single adults. The majority of of homeless people are living with a mental illness or another type(s) of health problem. African- Americans and latinos are disproportionately affected by homelessness. About 57% of people in shelters are African- Americans, 31% are latinx, 8% are white, and 4% are of unkown ethnicity. The number of homeless people sleeping in shelters has increased by 85% over the course of ten years. The main cause of homelessness is unaffordable housing. Other major reasons includes - eviction, doubled-up or severely overcrowded housing, domestic violence, job loss, and hazardous housing conditions.

Of all the 61,421 homeless people in New York,meaning about only 4,000 are seen in public. Nearly 1 in 128 New Yorkers is homeless, but only 1 in every 17 of these people are visible. Studies show that 60% percent of New Yorkers don't hvae enough emergency funds, that are enough to cover at least 3 months' expenses. Studies also show that 20% of New Yorkers are currently living under the poverty line; which is less 24,300 for a family of four. About 1 in 3 people living under the poverty line, are children.
